Marvin and Samantha Minott, owners of the Sankofayah clothing company realized that NOW is the time to reintroduce the power of group economics—especially for a people with the spending power of over 2 trillion dollars. DUWE (Diaspora Universal Welfare Economics) is the change agent that will create a new and consistent economic cycle, giving black people the power to truly build and stabilize our own financial backing. Through DUWE, consumers who love to “Shop Black” can actively participate in group economics with an infrastructure that financially aids in the development of our communities. Conversely, Black Business owners can expand their customer reach to increase profits and obtain business resources. “Black people must practice group economics to survive in this capitalistic society.” ~Claude Anderson
